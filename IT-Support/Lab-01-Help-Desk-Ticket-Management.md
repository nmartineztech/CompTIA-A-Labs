# Lab 01 – Help Desk Ticket Management

## Objective

Practice standard IT help desk procedures by managing support tickets, assisting users, documenting work performed, and escalating issues when appropriate.
---
## Skills Practiced

- Help desk operations
- Ticket prioritization
- Incident documentation
- Customer communication
- File recovery
- Ticket escalation
- Service Level Agreement (SLA) awareness
- Problem-solving
---
## Environment

- Simulated Windows enterprise environment
- Administrative workstation
- Help desk ticketing system
- End-user workstations

---

## Scenario

In this lab, I worked through several help desk requests that required reviewing ticket information, determining priority, resolving user issues when possible, documenting actions taken, and escalating issues that required higher-level support.

---

## Tasks Performed

### 1. Responded to a User Inquiry

- Reviewed an unassigned support request.
- Verified the information requested by the user.
- Responded using clear and professional communication.
- Updated the ticket with the resolution.
- Closed the ticket after confirming completion.
---
### 2. Assisted an End User

- Investigated a reported issue.
- Identified the cause of the problem.
- Restored a deleted file for the user.
- Confirmed the issue was resolved.
- Updated the ticket with the actions performed.
---
### 3. Escalated an Issue

- Reviewed remaining open tickets.
- Determined one issue was outside the scope of Level 1 support.
- Verified the reported problem.
- Escalated the ticket to the appropriate support team.
- Included troubleshooting notes to assist the next technician.
---
## Best Practices Followed

- Prioritized tickets based on urgency and business impact.
- Maintained accurate documentation throughout the troubleshooting process.
- Used professional and courteous communication.
- Escalated issues outside my level of responsibility.
- Updated ticket ownership and status throughout the workflow.
- Closed tickets only after confirming the issue had been resolved.
---
## What I Learned

This lab reinforced that effective IT support involves more than solving technical problems. Proper documentation, clear communication, and following established support procedures help ensure efficient teamwork and provide users with a positive support experience. I also learned the importance of recognizing when an issue should be escalated rather than attempting a fix beyond my level of responsibility.
